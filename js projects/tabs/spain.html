SPAIN

Gazpacho

HISTORY

Gazpacho, cold soup of Spanish cuisine, especially that of Andalusia. It is an ancient dish mentioned in Greek and Roman literature, although two of the main ingredients of the modern version, tomatoes and green peppers, were brought to Spain from the New World only in the 16th century.

MATERIALS

  • 2 lb. tomatoes, quartered.
  • 2 Persian cucumbers, peeled and chopped.
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, chopped.
  • 1 clove garlic, roughly chopped.
  • 2 tbsp. red wine vinegar or sherry vinegar.
  • 1/2 c. water.
  • 1/3 c. extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for pan and garnish.
  • Kosher salt Freshly ground black pepper.
  • B2 slices country bread, cubed.
  • 2 tbsp. thinly sliced basil.

RECIPE

Combine tomatoes, cucumbers, pepper, garlic, vinegar, and water in the bowl of a food processor or blender. Blend until smooth, then add oil and blend to combine. Taste and season with salt, pepper, and more vinegar if needed. Cover and refrigerate until chilled. Meanwhile, in a large skillet over medium heat, add enough oil to coat the bottom of the pan. Add bread and cook, stirring occasionally, until golden and crisp. Remove from heat, season with salt, and let cool. To serve, divide soup among bowls and top with basil, croutons, and a drizzle of oil.
