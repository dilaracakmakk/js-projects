HISTORY

The history of this onion soup dates back to the Roman Period. It was mostly called peasant food in the Roman Period, but took its current form after the 18th century. What gives the soup its real flavor is the caramelization of the onions added to it with Brandy or Sherry while it is cooked slowly.

MATERIALS

  • 4 medium onions.
  • 4-5 tablespoons of butter.
  • 2 tablespoons of flour.
  • 1/2 liter of water.
  • Gruyere Cheese or Cheddar Cheese.
  • Salt.
  • Spice.
  • French bread or toast pieces.
  • Bouillon (Optional).
  • White wine (optional).

RECIPE

Finely chop the onions, after the butter you put in the pot melts, add the onions and mix until they turn pink. Add the flour and continue mixing. Slowly add the boiled water, being careful not to clump the flour on top of it. (Those who will add bouillon can melt the bouillon in hot water first and add it) Those who want to add white wine can add the wine. You can increase the amount of water according to the density of the soup. Make sure it is not too watery or dark. Add salt and spices. Boil on low heat for about 30 minutes. Serve hot with Gruyere Cheese or Cheddar cheese and toasted bread crumbs before serving.
